HIKKADUWA TURTLE HATCHERY AND RESCURE CENTER

Sea Turtle Hatchery & Rescue Center


The Sea Turtle Hatchery and Rescue Centre is located a few kilometers north of the major Hikkaduwa village, with the goal of preserving some of the world's endangered turtle species. It is just one of many turtle hatcheries on the island's southern shore. It began as a community initiative to help preserve the biodiversity of the southern coast and has since evolved into a full-fledged hatchery and rescue center with devoted teams working around the clock to research, care for, and protect several turtle species. They take care of disabled turtle and especially provide shelter to the eggs that are laid on the beach. They then provide food and shelter to the baby turtles until they are of age and release them back to the water they belong to.


Things to do


You can see the baby turtles in action in their protected surroundings in this place. You can observe the eggs, their different patterns, and if you are fortunate enough you may be able to see a baby turtle hatch. If any baby turtles are matured enough to be released to the water you could grab the best opportunity of your life to do a service to nature by releasing a turtle back to its habitat.

Location

The hikkaduwa turtle hatchery and rescure center lies few kilometers north of the main hikkaduwa town
